# Task 5 – Cyber Safety Case Study: Phishing Emails

## Objective
The objective of this task is to understand phishing emails, how cybercriminals use them to scam users, and how individuals can protect themselves from online fraud.

---

## What are Phishing Emails?
Phishing emails are fake emails sent by scammers pretending to be trusted organizations such as banks, companies, or online services. Their goal is to trick users into sharing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or bank details.

These emails often look professional and create urgency to make users act quickly without thinking.

---

## Common Characteristics of Phishing Emails
- Unknown or suspicious sender address  
- Urgent messages such as “Account will be blocked”  
- Fake links asking for login details  
- Spelling or grammatical mistakes  
- Requests for personal or financial information  
- Attachments from unknown sources  

---

## Example Scenario
A student receives an email claiming to be from a bank asking to update KYC details immediately. The email includes a link that looks official. When the user clicks the link and enters login credentials, the information is stolen by scammers and misused.

---

## Risks of Phishing
- Financial loss  
- Account hacking  
- Identity theft  
- Data misuse  
- Loss of privacy  

---

## Prevention Measures
- Never click links from unknown emails.
- Always check the sender’s email address carefully.
- Do not share passwords, OTPs, or personal details.
- Verify messages through official websites.
- Avoid downloading unknown attachments.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
- Report suspicious emails immediately.
